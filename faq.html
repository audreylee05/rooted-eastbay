Frequently Asked Questions

Am I eligible for these programs? +
Eligibility varies by program. Use our screener above — it'll ask 3 quick questions and show you only the resources you likely qualify for. When in doubt, apply anyway; many programs have broader eligibility than people expect.
Do I need to be a citizen to access these resources? +
No. Many programs are available to all California residents regardless of immigration status, including emergency shelter, free clinics, food banks, and some CalFresh benefits for children. We note eligibility details on each resource card.
Is my information safe if I use this site? +
We do not collect, store, or share any personal information. Using our screener is anonymous — we never ask for your name, address, or any identifying details. We built this site for the community, not for data.
What if I speak limited English? +
Our most-used pages are available in Spanish. Many of the resources we list — including La Clínica de La Raza and county services — offer services in Spanish, Vietnamese, Tagalog, Cantonese, and more. Look for the language tag on each resource card.
How do I know these resources are up to date? +
We review and update our directory regularly. If you find a resource that is outdated, closed, or missing, please use the "Suggest a Resource" button at the bottom — your input directly improves the site for everyone.
Can I submit a resource that's not listed? +
Yes — and we'd love that. Use the contact form below. If you know of a food pantry, free clinic, or community program that should be here, tell us and we'll verify and add it.
