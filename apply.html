How to Apply — Step by Step

62% of surveyed users had abandoned an application mid-process. These guides fix that.

CalFresh (EBT Food Stamps)

CalFresh provides monthly funds loaded onto an EBT card you can use like a debit card at grocery stores, farmers markets, and some restaurants. A family of 4 can receive up to $973/month.

What You'll Need

  • Photo ID (driver's license, passport, or ID card)
  • Proof of address (utility bill, mail)
  • Proof of income (pay stubs, employer letter)
  • Social Security numbers for applicants
  • Immigration documents if applicable
1

Check your eligibility

A family of 3 earning under ~$2,311/month gross may qualify. Undocumented immigrants may qualify for children in the household.

2

Apply online at BenefitsCal.gov

Takes about 20 minutes. You can save and return. Available in Spanish, Vietnamese, Chinese, and more.

3

Complete your phone interview

You'll receive a call within 3-5 days to verify your information. Have your documents nearby.

4

Receive your EBT card

If approved, your card arrives in 7-10 business days. Funds are added monthly.

Medi-Cal (Free Health Insurance)

Medi-Cal is California's free or low-cost health insurance for low-income individuals and families. It covers doctor visits, hospital care, mental health, dental, prescriptions, and more.

What You'll Need

  • Proof of California residency
  • Proof of income or statement of no income
  • Social Security number (if you have one)
  • Immigration documents if applicable
  • Birth certificates for children
1

Apply at BenefitsCal.gov

You can apply online, by phone (1-800-300-1506), by mail, or in person at your county Social Services office.

2

Wait for determination

Alameda County typically processes applications within 45 days. Urgent medical need can be processed in 3 days.

3

Choose a health plan

You'll receive a letter to choose a Medi-Cal managed care plan. If you don't choose, one will be assigned.

4

Get your Medi-Cal card

Use it at any Medi-Cal provider. Find providers at Medi-Cal.ca.gov.

Apply at BenefitsCal.gov →

Free & Low-Cost Clinics

East Bay community health clinics provide care regardless of insurance status or ability to pay. Services include primary care, mental health, dental, women's health, and vaccinations.

Top East Bay Clinics

  • La Clínica de La Raza — Oakland, Fruitvale, Hayward
  • Roots Community Health Center — East Oakland
  • LifeLong Medical Care — Berkeley, Richmond
  • Axis Community Health — Pleasanton, Livermore
  • Alameda County Public Health — free vaccines
1

Find your nearest clinic

Use the filter above to find a clinic by city. Most accept walk-ins for urgent care; appointments for primary care.

2

Call ahead

Ask about sliding-scale fees, language support, and what services are available same-day.

3

Bring ID if you have it

Most clinics do not require insurance or ID. Bring whatever you have — they'll work with you.

4

Ask about additional benefits

Clinic staff can often help you apply for Medi-Cal or CalFresh on the spot.

Emergency Shelter Tonight

If you or someone you know needs shelter tonight, these East Bay resources provide immediate help. You do not need to be a citizen or have ID to access emergency shelter.

Key Contacts

  • Alameda County 211 — call or text 211
  • BOSS (Building Opportunities for Self-Sufficiency) Oakland
  • Building Futures — Hayward, San Leandro
  • Henry Robinson Center — Oakland men's shelter
  • Covenant House — youth ages 18-24, Oakland
1

Call 211 first

Alameda County 211 is a free, 24/7 hotline that will assess your situation and find the nearest available bed tonight.

2

For families with children

Call Alameda County Family Emergency Shelter at (510) 259-7285. Families are prioritized.

3

Ask about rapid rehousing

If you're in a shelter, ask about Alameda County's rapid rehousing program which can help you transition to permanent housing.

4

Rental assistance

Alameda County HOME program and state programs may cover back rent to prevent future evictions.
